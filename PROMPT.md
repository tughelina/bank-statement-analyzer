# 💎 Bank Statement Clarity — Your AI Prompt

*A gift to help you see your business finances clearly — in minutes, not days.*

## How to use this (3 steps)

1. **Open an AI that can read files and build spreadsheets.**
   Works with **Claude** (use the file/analysis feature) or **ChatGPT** (needs *Data Analysis / Code Interpreter*, included in the paid plans). Any AI that can (a) read your uploaded files and (b) create an Excel file will work. A plain chat without file upload can only give you tables as text — not a finished `.xlsx`.

2. **Upload your bank statements** for a full year — one file per month or one big file. CSV, Excel, or PDF all work (CSV/Excel give the cleanest result).

3. **Copy everything inside the grey box below**, paste it as your message, and send. Then answer the few short questions the AI asks you.

That's it. You'll get back one clean Excel workbook with everything sorted.

## ⬇️ Copy from here ⬇️

> **You are my financial-clarity assistant. I'm giving you my business bank statements for a full year (one or more files — CSV, Excel, or PDF). Turn them into ONE clean, professional Excel workbook (`.xlsx`) that gives me complete clarity on my money. Work carefully and accurately — never invent numbers.**
>
> **STEP 1 — Understand my data.**
> Read every file. Identify the columns: transaction date, description, amount (money in / money out), running balance, currency, and which account each row belongs to. Then tell me briefly what you found: the time period, total number of transactions, the accounts, and the currencies. If anything is unclear or a file looks incomplete, ask me before continuing.
>
> **STEP 2 — Ask me only the questions you truly need** (max 4), for example:
> - Which incoming payments are real **revenue from clients/customers**, versus money **I deposited myself** (owner deposits / capital)?
> - Which outgoing payments are my own **salary / draws** to a private account?
> - Which transfers are just **internal moves between my own accounts** (currency exchange, savings pockets) — not real income or expense?
> - In which **language and currency** should the final workbook be?
>
> **STEP 3 — Build the Excel workbook with these 6 sheets:**
>
> 1. **All Transactions** — every transaction, in date order. Columns: Date · Account · Currency · Type · Description · Income · Expense · Balance · Note. Tint income green and expense red. At the bottom, total Income and Expense **per currency**. (Important: if I have several accounts or currencies, do NOT merge them into one running balance — keep the bank's real balance per row, and total separately per currency.)
> 2. **Creditors — Detail** — every payment to a supplier/vendor, sorted alphabetically by creditor, then by date. Merge obvious variants of the same vendor into one name (e.g. different store/branch suffixes). Show refunds as negative values so the totals net correctly. Bundle all bank fees under a single creditor called "Bank Fees".
> 3. **Creditors — Summary** — one row per unique creditor: number of charges, total amount, first and last charge date. Alphabetical, with a grand total at the bottom.
> 4. **Debtors** — incoming payments that are **real revenue** (exclude my own deposits and internal moves). Give me a summary by payer plus a detail list. If revenue arrives in more than one currency, total each currency separately.
> 5. **Owner Account** — money flowing between me and my business: **Block A** = salary / draws (business → me), **Block B** = owner deposits (me → business), plus a clear **net figure** (draws − deposits).
> 6. **Internal Transfers** — moves between my own accounts (currency conversions, savings pockets) — no real money in or out.
>
> **RULES:**
> - Be precise and reconcile: every transaction must land in exactly **one** category. Show me the counts per category so I can confirm nothing was lost.
> - If any value or category is unclear, **ask me** — don't guess.
> - Keep each account's real bank balance per row; never mix currencies into a single total.
> - Make it clean and readable: bold headers, a frozen header row, filters on, and numbers formatted with thousands separators and 2 decimals.
> - Produce a **downloadable `.xlsx` file**. If you genuinely cannot create files, output each sheet as a clean table I can paste straight into Excel.
>
> **When you're done, give me a short summary:** total revenue, total to creditors, total salary/draws, and the reconciliation counts — so I can see the whole picture at a glance.

### A few tips for the best result
- **CSV or Excel files** give cleaner results than PDFs. If your bank offers a CSV export, use it.
- If you have **multiple accounts** (e.g. a main account, a savings pocket, or foreign-currency accounts), include them all — the prompt handles them.
- Answer the AI's questions honestly; that's where the magic of clean categories happens.
- Want it in your language? Just tell the AI in Step 2.
