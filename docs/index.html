The free prompt

Copy it, paste it into Claude or ChatGPT (with Data Analysis), upload your statements, and answer a few questions.

📋 Your prompt
You are my financial-clarity assistant. I'm giving you my business bank statements for a full year (one or more files — CSV, Excel, or PDF). Turn them into ONE clean, professional Excel workbook (.xlsx) that gives me complete clarity on my money. Work carefully and accurately — never invent numbers.

STEP 1 — Understand my data. Read every file. Identify the columns: transaction date, description, amount (money in / money out), running balance, currency, and which account each row belongs to. Then tell me briefly what you found: the time period, total number of transactions, the accounts, and the currencies. If anything is unclear, ask me before continuing.

STEP 2 — Ask me only the questions you truly need (max 4): Which incoming payments are real revenue from clients vs. money I deposited myself (owner deposits)? Which outgoing payments are my own salary / draws to a private account? Which transfers are just internal moves between my own accounts (currency exchange, savings pockets)? In which language and currency should the final workbook be?

STEP 3 — Build the Excel workbook with these 6 sheets:
1. All Transactions — every transaction, in date order. Columns: Date, Account, Currency, Type, Description, Income, Expense, Balance, Note. Tint income green, expense red. At the bottom, total Income and Expense per currency. If I have several accounts or currencies, do NOT merge them into one running balance — keep the bank's real balance per row and total per currency.
2. Creditors — Detail — every payment to a supplier, alphabetically by creditor then by date. Merge obvious variants of the same vendor into one name. Show refunds as negative so totals net. Bundle all bank fees under one creditor "Bank Fees".
3. Creditors — Summary — one row per creditor: number of charges, total, first and last charge date. Alphabetical, with a grand total.
4. Debtors — incoming payments that are real revenue (exclude owner deposits and internal moves). Summary by payer plus a detail list. Per-currency totals if multi-currency.
5. Owner Account — Block A: salary/draws (business to me), Block B: owner deposits (me to business), plus a net figure (draws minus deposits).
6. Internal Transfers — moves between my own accounts (currency conversions, savings pockets) — no real money in or out.

RULES: Be precise and reconcile — every transaction lands in exactly one category; show me the counts so nothing is lost. If unclear, ask — don't guess. Keep each account's real bank balance per row; never mix currencies into one total. Make it clean: bold headers, frozen header row, filters on, thousands separators with 2 decimals. Produce a downloadable .xlsx file; if you cannot create files, output each sheet as a clean table I can paste into Excel.

When done, give me a short summary: total revenue, total to creditors, total salary/draws, and the reconciliation counts.
